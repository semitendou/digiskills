What is Digital Skills for All?

+

Digital Skills for All is a community organization dedicated to empowering people of all ages with the knowledge and confidence to navigate the digital world safely and effectively. We provide free resources, workshops, and support to help bridge the digital divide in our communities.

Who can participate in your programs?

+

Our programs are designed for everyone! We offer specialized content for:

  • Seniors looking to build digital confidence
  • Young people learning to navigate social media safely
  • Parents wanting to understand digital parenting
  • Anyone interested in improving their digital literacy

All our programs are free and open to the community.

How much do your workshops cost?

+

All our workshops, resources, and programs are completely free! We believe that digital literacy should be accessible to everyone, regardless of their financial situation. Our funding comes from community grants, donations, and partnerships with local organizations.

Where are your workshops held?

+

We hold workshops at various community locations including:

  • Local libraries
  • Community centers
  • Senior centers
  • Schools and educational institutions
  • Online via video conferencing

Check our Latest News section or contact us for upcoming workshop locations and schedules.

How can I spot an online scam?

+

Here are key red flags to watch for:

  • Urgency: Messages that pressure you to act quickly
  • Too good to be true: Unrealistic offers or prizes
  • Requests for personal information: Legitimate organizations rarely ask for sensitive data via email
  • Poor grammar: Many scams contain spelling or grammatical errors
  • Suspicious links: Hover over links to see where they really lead

When in doubt, STOP and verify through official channels. Our Report a Scam page has more detailed guidance and resources.

What should I do if I think I've been scammed?

+

If you suspect you've been scammed, take these immediate steps:

  • Stop all communication with the suspected scammer
  • Contact your bank immediately if you shared financial information
  • Change your passwords for any compromised accounts
  • Report the incident to our scam reporting service
  • Document everything - save messages, emails, and screenshots

Remember, you're not alone. Many people experience scams, and there are resources available to help you recover and protect yourself in the future.

How do I create strong passwords?

+

Strong passwords are your first line of defense. Follow these guidelines:

  • Use at least 12 characters
  • Include uppercase and lowercase letters, numbers, and symbols
  • Avoid common words, names, or dates
  • Use a unique password for each account
  • Consider using a passphrase like "Coffee@Morning7Rain!"

Pro tip: Consider using a password manager to securely store and generate strong passwords. This way you only need to remember one master password.

I'm a senior and feel overwhelmed by technology. Where should I start?

+

It's completely normal to feel overwhelmed! We recommend starting with these basics:

  • Start small: Focus on one device or app at a time
  • Practice regularly: Even 10-15 minutes daily builds confidence
  • Learn with others: Join our senior-friendly workshops
  • Ask for help: Don't hesitate to ask questions - we love helping!

Our Seniors page has specially designed courses that start with the absolute basics and build up gradually. Remember, every expert was once a beginner!

How can I video call my grandchildren?

+

Video calling is a wonderful way to stay connected! Here are the most popular options:

  • WhatsApp: Free, works on smartphones, very popular
  • FaceTime: For Apple devices (iPhone, iPad, Mac)
  • Zoom: Works on any device, great for group calls
  • Facebook Messenger: If you use Facebook, this is built-in
  • Skype: Long-standing option, works on all devices

Our "Connecting with Family" course covers video calling step-by-step. We also offer one-on-one support to help you set up and practice with your specific device.

Is online banking safe for seniors?

+

Yes, online banking is generally very safe when you follow security best practices:

  • Official apps only: Download banking apps directly from official app stores
  • Secure networks: Only bank on trusted Wi-Fi networks (avoid public Wi-Fi)
  • Never share details: Banks never ask for passwords or PINs via email/phone
  • Check statements: Regularly review your accounts for unauthorized activity
  • Log out properly: Always log out when finished, especially on shared devices

Our "Digital Banking for Seniors" course teaches online banking safety step-by-step. Many seniors find online banking actually helps them monitor their finances more easily!

How can I help my teenager stay safe on social media?

+

Open communication is key! Here are some strategies:

  • Have regular conversations about online experiences
  • Set privacy settings together - make it a collaborative process
  • Teach them to think before posting - would they be comfortable with grandma seeing it?
  • Establish screen-free times for family connection
  • Model good behavior - they're watching how you use technology

Our Youth programs include resources for both teens and parents. We also offer family workshops where you can learn together!

What should young people know about online privacy?

+

Young people should understand that what they share online can have long-term consequences:

  • Digital footprint: Everything posted online leaves a permanent record
  • Privacy settings: Learn to control who sees what on each platform
  • Personal information: Never share addresses, phone numbers, or school details publicly
  • Photos: Be thoughtful about what images you share and where
  • Location sharing: Turn off location services for most apps

Our "Digital Citizenship for Youth" course covers these topics in detail, helping young people build healthy digital habits that will serve them throughout their lives.

What's the difference between Wi-Fi and mobile data?

+

Understanding the difference helps you stay connected and manage costs:

  • Wi-Fi: Internet from your home router or public hotspots - usually free/unlimited
  • Mobile Data: Internet through your phone plan - may have usage limits and costs

Best practice: Use Wi-Fi when available to save your mobile data. Most devices automatically connect to known Wi-Fi networks. Our "Understanding Internet Connections" course explains this in more detail.
